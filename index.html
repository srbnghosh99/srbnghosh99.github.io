I am a Ph.D. Candidate at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte, working with Dr. Erik Saule. I received my Master's in Computational Modeling and Simulaiton Engineering at Old Dominion University advised by Dr. Michel Audette working on Deformable Simplex Mesh. Currently my primary research interest intersects in Graph Mining, Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing (NLP), and Social Media Mining.

My research agenda is based on three pillars:

  • Graph Representation Learning: Understanding complex networks via structure-aware embeddings and downstream tasks like community detection, node classification, and link prediction.
  • Applied Machine Learning for Social Impact: Using NLP and network analysis to study transportation safety, hate speech, and misinformation propagation.
  • Scientometrics: Understanding scholarly collaboration networks via trends and patterns in research communities, topics and, bias & disparities interms of race and gender.
In my spare time, I practice yoga, write blogs. I enjoy cooking and love outdoor activities like hiking and trekking. When I can, I travel the world to learn about new cultures.
